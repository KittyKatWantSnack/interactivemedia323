  •         Far from the churchyard dig his grave,
  •                   On some green mound beside the wave;

  •                           To westward, sea and sky alone,

  •                   And sunsets. Put a mossy stone,
  •         With mortal name and date, a harp


  1.         And bunch of wild flowers, carven sharp;
  2.                   Then leave it free to winds that blow,

  3.                           And patient mosses creeping; slow,

  4.                   And wandering wings, and footsteps rare
  5.         Of human creature pausing there.
