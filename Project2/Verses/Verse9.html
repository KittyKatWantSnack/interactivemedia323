Far

from

the

churchyard

dig
his
grave,

On

some

green

mound

beside
the
wave;

To

westward,

sea

and

sky
alone,

And

sunsets.

Put

a

mossy
stone,

With

mortal

name

and

date,
a
harp

And

bunch

of

wild

flowers,
carven
sharp;

Then

leave

it

free

to
winds
that
blow,

And

patient

mosses

creeping;

slow,

And

wandering

wings,

and

footsteps
rare

Of

human

creature

pausing

there.
